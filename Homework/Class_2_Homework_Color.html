Red

Red can be aggressive and is also used as a warning color for cancel, delete or alert buttons/popups/text.

Orange

Orange like red can be aggressive but usually doesn't have the same negative connotations. A pretty modern accent color.

Yellow

Similar to orange but less useful in my opinion due to how hard it can be to see, especially in brighter layouts.

Green

A good color to lead people to something affirmative that you want them to interact with. Also useful like blue for a site-wide theme.

Blue

A great color to use everywhere without offending anybody.

Purple

Often used as a good accent to blue. Traditionally the color of clicked links, though not as important now.

White

Clean and when used as a foundation, allows you to focus on more important places. Provides a good base for high contrast with most other colors.

Gray

Similar to white - allows you to layer in low contrast to bring people's attention around the screen without having any sort of positive or negative connotation. Also useful for disabled buttons / features.

Black

Black text on a white background (or vice versa) provides the best contrast possible for reading.

Brown

Useful in a similar way as grey to provide a neutral background to content when tinted up or down. Warm and holistic.

Pink

Unfortunately it's pretty typecast as feminine. Deeper shades such as magenta can be useful to draw attention similar to red but without the negative connotation.
